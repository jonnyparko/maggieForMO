Meet Maggie!

ABOUT ME

As a mom and teacher in this community, I understand our need for candidates who will advocate for our Northland families and communities.

I am the product of great public education in the state of Missouri. I graduated from Smithville High School, received my bachelor’s degree at Truman State University and my Master’s in Education at the University of Missouri at Kansas City. After graduating from college, I served in the Peace Corps in Ecuador for two years, along with my husband Gregg, where our primary focus was on education and public health. I am a Spanish teacher a North Kansas City High School, teaching students in the International Baccalaureate Programme. I previously taught at University Academy in Kansas City, MO for five years. At Northtown, I’ve served as the sponsor of the Young Democrats, coached cross-country and served as the building representative for the National Education Association (NEA).

I am an active member of Northland Progress and I currently serve as the treasurer of the Oakwood Community Association. My family and I are parishioners at St. Charles Borromeo Catholic Church.

Gregg and I have three sons, Luke, Eli, and Henry. Being a mom is my greatest job of all and my kids have given me a renewed purpose to advocate for bright futures for kids all over Missouri.

Issues

My fight is our fight.
I am running for state representative to fight for workers’ rights, affordable healthcare, well-funded public education and a strong infrastructure.

Health Care

I believe healthcare is a right for all and not a privilege. I will advocate for affordable healthcare for people in all stages of life.

Workers' Rights

As a union member, I understand what it means to have our workers’ rights constantly under attack. I strongly opposed Prop A and worked hard to make sure that Right to Work was defeated. Once elected, I will continue this fight.

Education

We have great public schools in the Northland but I want to ensure that kids all over the state have access to high-quality public schools. I strongly oppose the expansion of charter schools outside of the boundaries of Kansas City and St. Louis.

Economic Growth

As a member of this community, I see every day the need for good, safe roads and bridges. We need to make sure our neighborhood streets have sidewalks, so kids can walk to school, and folks can get to work and do their shopping.
